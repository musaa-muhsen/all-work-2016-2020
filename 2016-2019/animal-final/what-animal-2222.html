Proboscis Monkey

Diet - Shoots, seeds and unripe fruits along with a passing insect on occasion.


Habitat - The island of borneo is the known location of the proboscis monkey. they live in the lower elevations of the forests and around the swamplands.


Life Span - 15-20 years.


Conservation Status - Endangered.


Fun Fact - The only fruit Proboscis Monkeys will eat is unripe fruit. the sugars in ripe fruits can ferment in their stomachs and cause fatal bloating.

Malayan Tapir

Diet - Grasses, leaves shoots, twigs and fruit.


Habitat - They live around water or forests in south-east asia.


Life Span - Around 30 years.


Conservation Status - Endangered.


Fun Facts - Tapirs have four toes on their front feet, and three toes on their back feet, they put food in their mouth using their noses. Just like an elephant.

Thirteen-lined Ground Squirrel

Diet - Seeds, fruits , roots, green vegetation and insects.


Habitat - Grasslands and prairies of north america.


Life Span - 2-3 years (7.9 years in captivity).


Conservation Status - Least concern.


Fun Facts - They are excellent diggers they build extensive burrows some for hiding which are short in length and there are lots of them and hibernating burrows that are alot deeper than any other burrow.

Okapi

Diet - Tree leaves, buds, grasses, ferns, fruits and fungi.


Habitat - Central Africa.


Life Span - 20 to 30 years.


Conservation Status - Endangered.


Fun Facts - Female okapis are slightly taller than male okapis. the okapis dark tongue is long enough to reach its eyes and ears.

Bat-eared Fox

Diet - Insects such ants, moths, eggs, chicks of birds, small rodents , reptiles and fungi.


Habitat - In short grass savannas and scrublands of eastern and southern africa.


Life Span - Around 13 years.


Conservation Status - Least concern.


Fun Fact - Unlike other dog , bat eared foxes don't seem to mind sharing their territory, up to 72 foxes have been recorded in 1 square mile.

Tarsier

Diet - Mostly insects diet along with small vertebrates such as frogs, lizards and small birds.


Habitat - Forest mangroves and scrub in a number of south-east Asian islands.


Life Span 12 - 20 years.


Conservation Status - Endangered.


Fun Fact - The tarsier is a unique animal as they are the only completely carnivorous primates in the world.
